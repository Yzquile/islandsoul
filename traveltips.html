TRANSPORTATION

How to Get to Siquijor

By Air

Fly to Dumaguete:

Book a flight to Dumaguete-Sibulan Airport (DGT) from major Philippine cities.

From Dumaguete to Siquijor:

Take a tricycle or taxi to Dumaguete Port, then board a ferry to Siquijor. The ferry ride takes approximately 1-2 hours.

By Land and Sea

Bus to Dumaguete:

If coming from Cebu City, take a bus to Liloan Port in Southern Cebu. From Liloan Port, take a ferry to Sibulan Port in Negros Oriental.

From Negros Oriental to Siquijor:

Take a tricycle or taxi to Dumaguete Port, then catch a ferry to Siquijor.

By Ferry from Other Islands

From Bohol:

Take a ferry from Tagbilaran City to Larena Port in Siquijor.

From Cebu:

Take a ferry from Cebu City or Liloan Port to Sibulan Port in Negros Oriental, then proceed to Siquijor.

TIPS: Check ferry schedules, consider weather conditions, and explore alternative routes.

GENERAL TRAVEL TIPS

Essential Travel Insights

Currency and Banking

Siquijor primarily uses the Philippine Peso (PHP). Ensure you have sufficient local currency for small purchases and transactions. ATMs are available in major towns, providing convenient access to cash. Notify your bank about your travel dates to avoid any issues with card transactions.

Language

While Filipino (Tagalog) is the official language, locals commonly speak Cebuano/Bisaya. English is widely understood, making communication relatively easy for travelers. Learn a few basic phrases in the local dialect to enhance your interactions and show appreciation for the culture.

Weather

Siquijor has a tropical climate with a dry season from November to April and a wet season from May to October. Pack light clothing, sunscreen, and a rain jacket if visiting during the wet season. Check weather forecasts before planning outdoor activities.

Respectful Travel Practices

Respect local traditions and practices by dressing modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Remove your shoes before entering homes or sacred places. Greet locals with a friendly "hello" or "kumusta" to foster positive interactions.

Health and Safety

Stay hydrated, especially in the tropical climate. Use sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun's rays. Be cautious with street food; choose vendors with clean and hygienic setups. Ensure your vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling to Siquijor.

Exploration Etiquette

Dispose of trash responsibly and participate in community initiatives promoting cleanliness and sustainability. Follow designated trails when hiking or trekking to minimize environmental impact. Capture memories with photographs but be mindful of local privacy and ask permission when necessary.

Connectivity and Communication

Check local SIM card options for your phone to stay connected during your visit. Wi-Fi is available in many accommodations and restaurants. Download offline maps or use navigation apps to explore the island without relying solely on data.

Safety First

While Siquijor is generally safe, be vigilant with your belongings. Keep important documents secure and be cautious in crowded areas. Familiarize yourself with emergency contact numbers and the location of the nearest medical facilities.

Cultural Immersion

Engage with locals to learn more about their culture and traditions. Attend local events or festivals if your visit coincides with any celebrations. Participate respectfully and savor the richness of Siquijor's cultural heritage.
